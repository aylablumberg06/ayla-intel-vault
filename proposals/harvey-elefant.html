§ 01 · The Opening
An honest memorandum.

Before we price a system, we price the relationship.

You reached out to me from a personal address. The firm name is Elefant International. The show you picked to theme your team was Succession. I’m going to assume you chose that on purpose, because Succession is a show about rooms — who gets in, who waits outside, and who gets to decide which story is told afterward.

This proposal is not a pitch. A pitch would be six agents, three prices, a Telegram bot. You’ve already seen that template from lesser firms. This memorandum is what I’d hand you if we met at the Carlyle for a forty-minute breakfast.

A deliberate disclosure. I researched Elefant International before writing this. There is no public website, registered address, or director filing I could verify in the time I had. The Gmail suffix suggests an operator who is either early, deliberately private, or both. Any of those is fine — but I’m not going to pretend I know more about your book of business than I do. The first thirty minutes of our first call will be me asking you seven or eight questions that no agent could answer from the outside.

What follows is a proposal for how I’d enter the engagement, not a quote for a shrink-wrapped product. Price comes after scope. Scope comes after the call.

§ 02 · Thesis

Partnership work rewards depth, not volume.

A direct-to-consumer operation wants 500 qualified emails a week. An international partnership shop wants ten. Of those ten, three will return the call. Of those three, one becomes a decade of revenue. The machine that wins this business is not the machine that sends the most — it is the machine that researches the deepest, drafts the best, and follows up on the exact day no one else does.

That is the engagement I’d design for Elefant. Low-volume. Bespoke. Every asset forged for a specific target in a specific market. The agents exist to do the reading, the filtering, and the first drafts — so your time only goes to decisions that actually move the number.

§ 04 · Discovery

Before we build, eight questions.

I’ll ask these on our first call. Your answers determine whether the engagement is worth your retainer and my time. If any of them feel out of bounds, that’s its own data.

Which market does Elefant primarily operate across — DACH, CEE, MENA, APAC, transatlantic? What’s the dominant corridor?
What is the average deal size — is a good quarter one closed partnership or ten?
Who is the archetypal ideal partner, by name? One company is enough.
How does Elefant earn revenue today — retainer, transaction fee, equity participation, referral?
Where does Elefant currently lose deals — in discovery, pricing, timing, or relationship?
How much of your week is spent on outbound you wish wasn’t on your desk?
What would you not want an agent to handle, ever? The work that has to stay yours.
What would “obviously worth it” look like, twelve months from now?
