02 · Where It Breaks

The real bottlenecks.

Every business in your shape hits the same four walls. They're the problems worth solving because solving them moves the number — not the vanity metrics.

Manual Etsy / Amazon / social scraping eats hours every week

You can't compete with SkinnyPixel or Chewy on ad budget — you win on finding the right owner before they even know they want it. That hunting takes time.

Generic copy lands in the noise

'Dog suspenders' is a product listing. The story — 'suspenders that keep your pup's boots on through Dallas winters' — is the pitch that converts. You need that written every week for a different breed.

One-time buyers don't come back

A dog owner buys one pair. Then what? No follow-up means no reviews, no referrals, no second purchase for the next puppy. Retention is where niche products make real money.

Retail buyer outreach is brutal

Pet boutiques and vet offices are the wholesale channel that matters — and they only open emails that feel handwritten. No BDR, no budget, no way to scale that manually.
