The Honest Opening

This one isn’t a cold pitch.

You already know what I built. You’ve been watching. But putting Design List Collective on the same page as my other proposals felt wrong — so this one’s different. No BDR framing. No generic pain points. Just the version of this I’d pitch you over coffee if you weren’t my mom.

The premise is simple: The List works because the three of you make every match personally. That’s the product. The problem is that the three of you can’t personally do six things at once — research, outreach, intake, designer-side account management, content, reporting — and every hour spent on the stuff below the matching itself is an hour The List doesn’t grow.

The agents aren’t here to replace your judgment. They’re here to hand you better briefs, faster — so you’re spending more time on the part of this business only you can do.

The Team

Six agents, cast from St Elmo’s Fire.

You picked the show, so the names stick — but every role is sized for how The List actually runs today. Dallas-first. Human-facing. Quality over volume.

01

Billy — the dallas homeowner finder

Lead Research

Watches real-estate signals (Zestimate jumps, recent closings, permit pulls in 75205/75209) plus Houzz saves, Nextdoor threads, and Pinterest boards to surface Dallas homeowners actually in-market. Delivers a curated weekly list — never a dump.

Preston Hollow, Highland Park, Lakewood
02

Jules — the intake interpreter

Client Research

Takes the 6-minute Find Your Match form, cross-references it against lifestyle signals, and writes a one-page brief for each homeowner. Project scope, aesthetic leaning, phase of life, capacity signals. The brief Wendy needs to pick right.

Turns forms into portraits
03

Wendy — the matchmaker

Designer Match

Reads Jules’ brief against all sixteen pros — Yates Desygn’s livability-first philosophy, Sey Interiors, Studio EL73’s architecture-first approach, Neighbor Interiors’ laid-back luxury, Gaia G’s modern warmth. Picks the match and explains why — the way you would on a call.

Knows the roster cold
04

Kevin — the presenter

Introduction Writing

Crafts the match intro in Afrat’s voice. Leads with the specific designer quality the homeowner will feel — “architecture shaped around how you actually live” lands differently for a new-build family than for a historic-home restorer.

Writes like Afrat
05

Alec — the quality gate

Editorial Review

Final read before any match goes out. Style fit, capacity, chemistry signal. If it’s wrong, Alec kills it. Nothing moves to Kirby without his sign-off — which is to say, without your standard on record.

Sharon’s standard, automated
06

Kirby — the connector

Warm Introductions

Warm email + text intros. Schedules the first consult. Checks in with both sides after and writes the outcome back to Wendy — so the matching sharpens every cycle. The List gets smarter while you sleep.

Closes the loop
